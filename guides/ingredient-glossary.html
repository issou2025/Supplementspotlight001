The Ultimate Supplement Ingredient Glossary

Demystify supplement labels with our A-Z guide to the most common ingredients in sports nutrition.

A B C D

A

Ashwagandha

What it is: An adaptogenic herb used in traditional medicine.

What it does: Primarily helps the body manage stress by lowering cortisol levels. May also improve strength and recovery.

Common Dosage: 300-600 mg per day.

B

Beta-Alanine

What it is: A non-essential amino acid.

What it does: Increases carnosine levels in muscles, which buffers acid and delays fatigue during high-intensity exercise (the "tingles" are a common, harmless side effect).

Common Dosage: 3.2-6.4 grams per day.

C

Creatine Monohydrate

What it is: One of the most studied and effective supplements in history.

What it does: Helps muscles produce energy (ATP) during heavy lifting or high-intensity exercise, leading to increased strength, power, and muscle mass.

Common Dosage: 3-5 grams per day.

L-Citrulline

What it is: An amino acid that converts to L-Arginine in the kidneys.

What it does: Boosts nitric oxide production more effectively than arginine itself, leading to improved blood flow, better muscle "pumps," and enhanced endurance.

Common Dosage: 6-8 grams (as Citrulline Malate) 30-60 minutes before exercise.
